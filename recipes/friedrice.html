Nigerian Fried Rice.

This is a foolproof recipe for cooking Jollof rice, West Africa's most popular dish.
I like to serve it as a side with my West African-style grilled beef (Suya), but it can also be a main course.

Ingridients

  • Chicken Stock
  • Peppers (scotched bonnets/Habanero, fresno chili)
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Red Bell papers
  • Spices (salt, chicken bouillon thyme, curry powder, paprika, cumin, tumeric)
  • olive oil
  • Rice

Steps:

  1. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat.
    Add onion and salt and saut√© until onion starts to soften, 4 to 5 minutes. Clear space in the middle of the pan,
    add chicken stock.
    Season with paprika, curry powder, cumin, thyme, pepper, and turmeric;
    continue cooking for 1 to 2 minutes until everything is well combined.
  2. Turn off the heat, and stir in the rice, making sure every grain of rice is coated with the oil-chicken stock mixture.
    Add bay leaf and chicken bouillon paste.
  3. Turn heat to high, stir in chicken stock sauce, and bring to a vigorous simmer.
    Cover with a tight fitting lid, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer for 20 minutes. Do not remove the lid or stir rice.
  4. Turn off heat after 20 minutes, and set the timer for 12 minutes. Allow rice to sit and do not remove the lid.
  5. After the 12 minutes are up, remove lid, and fluff rice with a fork, separating all the grains. Serve garnished with green onions and chopped cilantro.
